React to the new model

A

Immediate move (no confirmation gate)

When you drag or pick "Move to future," the move applies instantly — no preview modal, no "are you sure." Validation runs after; flags appear if anything broke. Light, responsive, easy to iterate.

B

Red flag badge on broken tiles

Affected tiles get a red ring + small "!" badge in the corner. Hover/click reveals the specific issue.

C

Top banner with issue summary

Persistent banner above the plan view: red when issues exist, green when clean. Has Show-all and Get-help buttons. The "Get help" button is what opens the wizard (Mode B from the spec) for users who want batch assistance.

D

Suggested-fix card at top of action menu when invalid

When you click a flagged tile, the action menu opens with a green "Suggested fix" card at the very top — the most likely resolution one click away.

E

Inline destination picker for "Move to future"

Instead of opening another panel, "Move to future" expands inline with semester buttons. Recommended destination is highlighted green.

The wizard / cascade preview from the previous mockup isn't gone — it just lives behind the banner's "Get help" button for users who want batched, guided multi-step planning. Default flow stays simple.

Cascade-size handling — react to:

F

Banner adapts to cascade size

1-2 issues: thin banner with "Show all" + "Get help" (Mode B wizard). 3+ issues: tall banner with bold issue count, "Auto-Fix all" as dominant green action, plus the secondary buttons.

G

Auto-Fix = one click runs the cascade engine

Engine pushes every broken-prereq course to its next valid semester. May push graduation back. May leave new empty slots that need filling. Tiles that got moved by Auto-Fix get a green ring + "⤴ Auto-Fixed" annotation so you can see what changed.

H

Undo Auto-Fix is a one-click revert

Right after Auto-Fix, the banner offers "Undo Auto-Fix" alongside "Looks good." A single Plan-snapshot-and-restore — only valid for the most recent Auto-Fix, not multi-level undo.

I

Graduation impact called out

If Auto-Fix pushes a course past Sem 8, a new semester appears (Sem 9 in this example) and the header updates: "grad: Fall 2030" — with a callout in the banner explaining the shift.

This pattern reuses the cascade engine from the spec — what's new is the UI affordance. The engine runs on every move; the banner just changes shape based on issue count.
